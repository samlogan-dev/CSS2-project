# Remote Work Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-002
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Last Updated:** February 2025

---

## Overview

This policy governs remote and hybrid work arrangements for all employees. The company supports flexible working as a means of improving employee wellbeing and productivity, provided that business outcomes and team collaboration are not compromised.

---

## Eligibility

Remote work arrangements are available to employees who:

- Have completed at least 3 months of continuous employment.
- Are not in a role that requires on-site presence (e.g., facilities, on-site support).
- Have a suitable home working environment (see requirements below).
- Are not currently subject to a performance improvement plan.

New employees are expected to work on-site full-time during their first 3 months to support effective onboarding and relationship building. Refer to `guide-onboarding.md` for details on the onboarding period.

---

## Hybrid Work Model

The default arrangement is a hybrid model: **3 days on-site, 2 days remote** per week. Specific on-site days are agreed between the employee and their line manager based on team rhythms (e.g., stand-ups, sprint planning, all-hands).

Employees wishing to work remotely more than 2 days per week must submit a formal flexible work request via the HR portal, which will be assessed on a case-by-case basis.

---

## Home Working Environment Requirements

Employees working remotely must have:

1. A stable internet connection of at least 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload.
2. A dedicated workspace free from significant distractions during core hours.
3. Company-issued equipment or personally owned equipment that meets IT security requirements (see `guide-it-setup.md`).
4. The ability to participate in video calls with camera enabled during team meetings.

---

## Core Hours

Regardless of location, all employees must be reachable and responsive during **core hours: 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM (local time)**. Outside of core hours, flexible scheduling is permitted provided that daily deliverables are met.

---

## Equipment and Expenses

The company will provide a one-time remote work allowance of $500 to cover home office setup costs (e.g., monitor, keyboard, chair). Receipts must be submitted via the expense system within 60 days of purchase. See `policy-expenses.md` for the reimbursement process.

Ongoing internet costs are the employee's responsibility and are not reimbursable.

---

## Security and Data Handling

Employees must comply with all IT security requirements when working remotely:

- Use of the company VPN is mandatory when accessing internal systems.
- Company data must never be stored on personal cloud storage (e.g., personal Dropbox, iCloud).
- Employees must lock their screen whenever they leave their workspace.
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately to the IT helpdesk (see `faq-it.md`).

---

## Monitoring and Accountability

Remote work is based on trust and outcomes. Managers may not use invasive monitoring software. Performance is assessed against agreed deliverables and objectives, not hours logged.

---

## Revocation

The company reserves the right to revoke remote work arrangements with 5 business days' notice if:

- Business needs require consistent on-site presence.
- The employee's performance or conduct has been affected by remote work.
- The employee is found to have violated this policy or related IT security policies.
