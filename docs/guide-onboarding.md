# New Employee Onboarding Guide

**Document ID:** HR-GUIDE-001
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Welcome

Welcome to the team. This guide walks you through everything you need to do in your first 4 weeks. If you have questions at any point, your assigned onboarding buddy or your People & Culture contact can help.

---

## Before Day One

Your manager and People & Culture should have already:

- Sent your employment contract and tax forms.
- Provisioned your company email address.
- Ordered your equipment (laptop, peripherals).

If you have not received your equipment by the day before your start date, contact people@company.com immediately.

---

## Week 1 — Getting Set Up

### Day 1

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00 AM | Meet your manager and receive your equipment |
| 10:00 AM | Complete IT setup (see `guide-it-setup.md` for step-by-step instructions) |
| 11:30 AM | HR orientation with People & Culture |
| 1:00 PM | Lunch with your team |
| 2:30 PM | Complete mandatory compliance training (links in your welcome email) |

### By End of Week 1

- [ ] IT setup complete: laptop, VPN, email, Slack, and core tools installed.
- [ ] Completed all mandatory compliance training modules.
- [ ] Read the Code of Conduct (`policy-code-of-conduct.md`).
- [ ] Read the Leave Policy (`policy-leave.md`) and understand your entitlements.
- [ ] Had a 1:1 with your manager to discuss your 30/60/90 day goals.
- [ ] Met your onboarding buddy.

---

## Week 2 — Learning the Business

By the end of Week 2, you should:

- Have attended your team's regular ceremonies (stand-up, sprint planning, retro).
- Completed at least one introductory task or ticket with support from a colleague.
- Met with 3–5 stakeholders outside your immediate team (your manager will arrange these).
- Enrolled in any role-specific training identified in your onboarding plan.

---

## Week 3 — Contributing

By the end of Week 3, you should:

- Be contributing to team work independently on tasks of low-to-medium complexity.
- Have a clear understanding of the team's current priorities and roadmap.
- Understand how to raise blockers and escalate issues within the team.

---

## Week 4 — Review

At the end of Week 4, you will have a formal check-in with your manager and a People & Culture representative to review:

- Progress against 30-day goals.
- Any support needs or blockers.
- Feedback from your onboarding buddy and colleagues.

This meeting is also an opportunity to ask questions about any policies or processes that are still unclear.

---

## Remote Work During Onboarding

New employees are expected to work on-site full-time during their first 3 months. This is to support effective relationship-building and faster onboarding. After 3 months, you may apply for a hybrid arrangement as described in `policy-remote-work.md`.

---

## Probation Period

All new employees serve a 3-month probation period. During this time:

- Either party may terminate employment with 1 week's notice.
- Leave entitlements apply from Day 1 (see `policy-leave.md`), but annual leave must be approved by your manager and may be limited during probation.
- A formal probation review meeting will be held at the end of Week 12.
