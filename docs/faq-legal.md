## Contracts and Vendor Agreements

### I need to sign a contract with a vendor. What's the process?

All contracts committing the company to external obligations must be reviewed by Legal before signing. Submit the draft agreement to legal@company.com with a brief summary of the commercial context. Legal will review within **5 business days** for standard agreements and **2 business days** for urgent matters. Do not sign or countersign any agreement without Legal sign-off.

### Can I use a vendor's standard contract template?

You may accept a vendor's template for low-value, low-risk engagements (under $5,000 and no data sharing). For all other agreements, Legal will review and negotiate the terms. If the vendor's template contains unusual liability, IP, or data processing clauses, escalate to Legal regardless of value.

### What is the company's standard payment term for vendor contracts?

The standard payment term is **net 30 days**. Any deviation (e.g., net 60, upfront payment) requires Finance approval. See `faq-finance.md` for procurement processes.

### Where are signed contracts stored?

All executed contracts are stored in the Legal repository (accessible via `legal.internal/contracts`). After signing, email a copy to legal@company.com so it can be catalogued. Do not store signed contracts only on your personal drive or in Slack.

### A vendor is asking us to sign an NDA. Is that okay?

Standard mutual NDAs with reputable vendors are generally fine. Forward the NDA to legal@company.com for a quick review — Legal can usually turn these around within 1 business day. Do not sign NDAs that are one-sided (protecting only the vendor) without Legal review.

## Data Privacy and Protection

### What are our obligations under the Privacy Act and Australian Privacy Principles?

The company is bound by the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) under the Privacy Act 1988. In summary, we must: collect personal information only for a lawful purpose; store it securely; not disclose it to third parties without consent (with exceptions); and allow individuals to access and correct their information on request. The full Privacy Policy is at `legal.internal/policies/privacy`.

### A customer has asked to access or delete their personal data. What do I do?

Forward the request to legal@company.com immediately. Under the APPs, we must respond within **30 days**. Legal will coordinate the response with the relevant data owners. Do not attempt to fulfil or decline a data access request without Legal involvement.

### We have suffered a potential data breach. What do we do?

Notify Legal immediately — do not wait to confirm the breach. Under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, we may have a **30-day** window to assess the breach and notify the OAIC and affected individuals. Early escalation is critical.

For security incidents involving data, also follow `brief-project-sentinel.md` — the Comms Lead is required to notify Legal within 30 minutes of a P1 security incident.

### Can we send personal data to overseas vendors?

Transfers of personal information outside Australia must comply with APP 8. Before engaging an overseas vendor who will process personal data, submit a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) request to legal@company.com. Legal will assess the vendor's privacy framework and, if necessary, execute a DPA.

### What counts as "personal information"?

Under the Privacy Act, personal information is any information or opinion about an identified individual or an individual who is reasonably identifiable. This includes names, email addresses, IP addresses, employment records, and health information. When in doubt, treat information as personal and consult Legal.

## Intellectual Property

### Who owns work I create as part of my job?

Work created by employees in the course of their employment is owned by the company. This includes code, documents, designs, and other work product created during working hours or using company resources. Your employment contract contains specific IP assignment clauses.

### What about work I create outside of work hours?

If the work is unrelated to your employment and you did not use company resources, it is generally yours. However, if the work relates to the company's business, industry, or technology, consult Legal before commercialising or publishing it.

### Can I use open-source software in our products?

Yes, but with care. Some open-source licences (e.g., GPL) have "copyleft" requirements that may affect how our products can be distributed. Before incorporating a new open-source library, check its licence and raise any GPL or AGPL licences with Legal before use. MIT, Apache 2.0, and BSD licences are generally acceptable.

### A third party claims we are infringing their IP. What do we do?

Escalate to legal@company.com immediately. Do not respond to the third party, acknowledge infringement, or make any admissions. Legal will assess the claim and coordinate the response.

## Compliance and Regulatory

### What are our obligations around employment law?

The company operates under the Fair Work Act and applicable modern awards. People & Culture manages compliance with employment law. For specific questions about employment obligations, contact people@company.com. For formal disputes or legal proceedings, Legal must be involved.

### We are being asked to respond to a regulatory request or government inquiry. What should we do?

Contact legal@company.com immediately. Do not respond directly to regulatory bodies, provide documents, or make statements without Legal involvement. This includes requests from the ATO, OAIC, ASIC, or any other regulator.

### Where can I find the company's key compliance policies?

Key policies are available on the internal wiki at `legal.internal/policies`. Core documents include:
- Privacy Policy
- Code of Conduct: `policy-code-of-conduct.md`
- Whistleblower Policy
- Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy
- Conflict of Interest Policy

If you cannot access a policy you need, contact legal@company.com.
