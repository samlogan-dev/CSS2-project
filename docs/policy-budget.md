# Budget Management Policy

**Document ID:** FIN-POL-001
**Owner:** Finance
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Overview

This policy governs the allocation, management, and reporting of departmental budgets at ACME Corporation. All department heads and project leads are required to comply with this policy. Budget management is a shared responsibility between Finance and department leadership.

---

## Section 1: Budget Cycle

1.1 The annual budget cycle runs in alignment with ACME Corporation's financial year (1 July – 30 June).

1.2 **Budget planning timeline:**

| Activity | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| Finance issues budget templates to department heads | April |
| Departments submit budget proposals | End of April |
| Finance consolidates and reviews proposals | May |
| Budget review meetings with department heads | May |
| Board approval of consolidated budget | June |
| Approved budgets communicated to departments | 1 July |

1.3 Budgets are set annually but reviewed quarterly. Mid-year budget adjustments require Head of Finance approval and a written business justification.

---

## Section 2: Budget Categories

2.1 All departmental budgets are structured across the following categories:

- **Headcount (HC)** — Salaries, superannuation, and on-costs for permanent and contract staff.
- **Opex — Technology** — Software licences, SaaS subscriptions, cloud infrastructure costs.
- **Opex — Travel and Entertainment** — Business travel, accommodation, and client entertainment. See `policy-expenses.md`.
- **Opex — Training and Development** — External training, conferences, and professional memberships.
- **Opex — General** — All other operating expenditure not covered above.
- **Capex** — Capital expenditure for assets with a useful life exceeding 12 months. All Capex items above AUD $10,000 require Board approval.

2.2 Budget transfers between categories within the same department require Head of Finance approval. Transfers between departments require approval from both department heads and the Head of Finance.

---

## Section 3: Spending Authorities

3.1 The following spending authorities apply to all non-headcount expenditure:

| Approver | Authority Limit |
|----------|----------------|
| Employee | Up to AUD $500 (with manager approval) |
| Manager / Team Lead | Up to AUD $5,000 |
| Department Head | Up to AUD $25,000 |
| Head of Finance | Up to AUD $100,000 |
| CEO | Up to AUD $500,000 |
| Board | Above AUD $500,000 |

3.2 Spending authorities apply per transaction. Splitting a single purchase across multiple transactions to avoid a higher approval threshold is a breach of this policy and the Code of Conduct — see `policy-code-of-conduct.md`.

3.3 All expenditure must be within the approved departmental budget. Expenditure that would cause the department to exceed its approved budget requires Head of Finance approval before commitment.

---

## Section 4: Project Budgets

4.1 Projects with a total budget above AUD $50,000 require a formal project brief approved by the steering committee before expenditure is committed. See `brief-project-atlas.md` and `brief-project-sentinel.md` for examples.

4.2 Project leads are responsible for tracking project expenditure against the approved budget on a monthly basis and reporting variances to the Head of Finance.

4.3 Project budget variances of more than 10% of the approved budget must be escalated to the steering committee within 5 business days of identification.

4.4 At project closeout, the project lead must submit a final budget reconciliation to Finance within 20 business days.

---

## Section 5: Financial Reporting

5.1 Monthly financial reports are distributed to department heads within 10 business days of month end. Reports include:
- Actual expenditure vs. budget by category
- Year-to-date actuals and forecast to year end
- Variance commentary for items exceeding 15% of budget

5.2 Department heads are required to provide written variance commentary to Finance within 5 business days of receiving the monthly report if any category exceeds 15% variance.

5.3 Quarterly business reviews include a formal Finance presentation to the leadership team covering consolidated company performance against budget.

---

## Section 6: Procurement

6.1 All new supplier relationships with an estimated annual spend above AUD $10,000 require a competitive tender process with a minimum of 3 quotes, unless a sole-source justification is approved by the Head of Finance.

6.2 All supplier contracts must be reviewed by Legal before signing. Contracts above AUD $100,000 require the CEO's signature.

6.3 Preferred suppliers are listed in the Finance system. Employees should use preferred suppliers where available, as these have pre-negotiated terms and pricing.

---

## Section 7: Compliance and Audit

7.1 Finance conducts a quarterly internal audit of a sample of expense claims and purchase orders across all departments.

7.2 Employees found to have submitted fraudulent claims, split transactions to circumvent spending limits, or otherwise breached this policy may face disciplinary action up to and including termination — see `policy-code-of-conduct.md`.

7.3 ACME Corporation's external auditors conduct an annual review of financial controls. Department heads may be required to provide supporting documentation during this process.
