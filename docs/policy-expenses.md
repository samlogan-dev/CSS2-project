# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-003
**Owner:** Finance
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Overview

This policy covers the reimbursement of legitimate business expenses incurred by employees while performing their duties. Expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and pre-approved where required.

---

## General Principles

- All expense claims must be submitted with original receipts (digital or physical).
- Claims must be submitted within **60 days** of the expense being incurred. Claims submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed without written approval from the Finance Director.
- Personal expenses disguised as business expenses are a disciplinary matter and may result in termination.
- Reimbursements are processed in the next payroll cycle after approval.

---

## Travel

### Flights

Economy class is the default for all flights under 6 hours. Business class may be approved for flights over 6 hours with prior written approval from the employee's Director. Bookings must be made through the company's preferred travel provider (see the Finance intranet page for the current provider and booking portal).

### Accommodation

The nightly accommodation cap is **$250 AUD** in major cities and **$180 AUD** in regional areas. Stays exceeding the cap require prior approval. The company negotiates corporate rates with several hotel chains — use of these rates is encouraged.

### Ground Transport

Taxis, rideshares, and public transport for business purposes are reimbursable. Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the ATO approved rate of **$0.88 per km**. Employees must log the start and end points of each journey.

### Meals and Per Diem

When travelling overnight for business, employees may claim a daily meal allowance of up to **$80 AUD per day** (breakfast included in accommodation excluded). Individual meal receipts must be provided.

---

## Client Entertainment

Client entertainment (meals, events, etc.) requires prior approval from a Director or above. Claims must include the names and organisations of all attendees. The per-person cap is **$150 AUD** without Director approval and **$300 AUD** with approval.

---

## Equipment and Subscriptions

Equipment purchases under **$500 AUD** may be expensed directly. Purchases above $500 must go through the procurement process — contact Finance before purchasing. Software subscriptions must be approved by IT before purchase, regardless of cost.

Employees setting up a home office under the Remote Work Policy (`policy-remote-work.md`) are entitled to a one-time $500 setup allowance, which is claimed through this expense process.

---

## Mobile Phone

Employees with a business mobile allowance (as specified in their employment contract) may claim up to **$60 AUD per month** toward their personal mobile plan. The claim is made by submitting a monthly bill showing the total amount.

---

## How to Submit a Claim

1. Log into the Finance portal at `finance.internal/expenses`.
2. Create a new expense report and select the appropriate category.
3. Upload receipts for each line item.
4. Submit to your line manager for approval.
5. Once approved, the report routes automatically to Finance for processing.

For assistance with the portal, refer to `faq-it.md` or contact the Finance helpdesk at finance-help@company.com.

---

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable under any circumstances:

- Alcohol (except when included as part of an approved client entertainment claim).
- Personal grooming, clothing, or gym memberships.
- Traffic infringements or parking fines.
- Spouse or partner travel costs.
- First-class or premium economy upgrades not pre-approved.
- Expenses older than 60 days without Finance Director sign-off.
