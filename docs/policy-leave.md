# Leave Policy

**Document ID:** HR-POL-001
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Annual Leave

All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees accrue leave on a pro-rata basis relative to their contracted hours.

Leave accrues from the employee's start date. Employees may not take annual leave in excess of their accrued balance without written approval from their line manager and the Head of People & Culture.

### Carrying Over Leave

Up to 5 days of unused annual leave may be carried over into the following calendar year. Any balance above 5 days is forfeited on 31 December unless a written exemption has been granted. Exemptions are only granted in cases where business needs prevented the employee from taking leave.

### Booking Leave

Annual leave must be requested via the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance for requests of 1–3 days, and at least 10 business days in advance for requests of 4 or more consecutive days. Leave requests are approved at the discretion of the line manager, who must consider team capacity and project deadlines before approving.

---

## Sick Leave

Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not accrue — the full entitlement resets on 1 January each year.

For absences of 1–2 days, employees must notify their line manager before 9:30 AM on the first day of absence. For absences of 3 or more consecutive days, a medical certificate is required and must be submitted to the HR portal within 5 business days of returning to work.

Sick leave taken in excess of the 10-day entitlement will be treated as unpaid leave unless the employee has accumulated personal leave credits.

---

## Parental Leave

Primary carers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary carers are entitled to 4 weeks of paid parental leave. Leave must commence within 12 months of the birth or adoption of the child.

Employees must notify People & Culture at least 8 weeks before the intended start date. See the **Onboarding & HR FAQ** (`faq-hr.md`) for information on how to apply.

---

## Compassionate Leave

Employees are entitled to up to 3 days of paid compassionate leave per incident in the event of the death or serious illness of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or sibling). Additional unpaid leave may be requested via the line manager.

---

## Study Leave

Employees enrolled in an approved professional development course may apply for up to 5 days of paid study leave per calendar year. Approval is at the discretion of the line manager. Study leave is separate from annual leave and must not be deducted from the annual leave balance.

---

## Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays without deduction from any leave balance. If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they will receive a substitute day off to be taken within 30 days, in addition to any applicable penalty rates as per their employment contract.

---

## Leave Without Pay

Employees may apply for leave without pay (LWOP) for periods up to 12 weeks. LWOP does not accrue annual or sick leave entitlements during the period of absence. Approval requires sign-off from the line manager and the Head of People & Culture.
