# Code of Conduct

**Document ID:** HR-POL-004
**Owner:** People & Culture
**Last Updated:** January 2025

---

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct sets the standard for professional behaviour expected of all employees, contractors, and representatives of the company. It applies in all work settings — on-site, remote, and at external events.

---

## Core Values

Our workplace is built on four principles:

1. **Respect** — Treat every person with dignity, regardless of their role, background, or identity.
2. **Integrity** — Act honestly and transparently in all professional dealings.
3. **Accountability** — Take ownership of your work and your mistakes.
4. **Collaboration** — Share knowledge, support colleagues, and work toward shared goals.

---

## Expected Behaviours

### Professional Conduct

- Communicate respectfully in all channels — in person, via email, Slack, video calls, and in written documents.
- Meet agreed deadlines and communicate proactively if blockers arise.
- Represent the company appropriately when dealing with clients, vendors, or the public.
- Maintain confidentiality of company information, client data, and colleague personal information.

### Inclusion and Diversity

The company is committed to a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and bullying. This includes behaviour related to:

- Race, ethnicity, or national origin
- Gender identity or expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age, disability, or health status
- Religion or political views

All employees are responsible for contributing to an inclusive environment. Bystander intervention — speaking up when witnessing unacceptable behaviour — is encouraged and supported.

### Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any actual or perceived conflicts of interest to their line manager and People & Culture. This includes personal relationships with vendors, investments in competitors, or secondary employment that may affect objectivity.

### Gifts and Hospitality

Employees must not solicit gifts or hospitality from clients or vendors. Unsolicited gifts valued over **$50 AUD** must be declared to your manager. Gifts in excess of $150 AUD must be declined or surrendered to the company.

---

## Prohibited Behaviours

The following behaviours are grounds for disciplinary action, up to and including termination:

- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any kind.
- Discrimination based on any protected attribute.
- Misuse of company assets, systems, or data.
- Falsifying records, timesheets, or expense claims (see `policy-expenses.md`).
- Sharing confidential company information without authorisation.
- Engaging in criminal conduct that affects the company or colleagues.
- Serious or repeated violations of any company policy.

---

## Reporting a Concern

If you witness or experience behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, you are encouraged to report it. Options include:

1. **Speak to your line manager** — for matters that do not involve your manager.
2. **Contact People & Culture directly** — people@company.com.
3. **Use the anonymous reporting tool** — available on the HR portal.

All reports are treated confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who raises a genuine concern in good faith is itself a disciplinary matter.

---

## Investigations

Reported concerns will be investigated promptly and fairly. Employees subject to an investigation are entitled to be informed of the allegations, to respond, and to have a support person present in any formal meeting.

---

## Disciplinary Process

Minor breaches may be addressed informally. Serious or repeated breaches will follow a formal disciplinary process:

1. Written notification of the alleged breach.
2. Opportunity to respond (in writing and/or in a meeting).
3. Decision and outcome communicated in writing.

Outcomes may include a formal warning, a performance improvement plan, demotion, or termination. Summary dismissal (without notice) is reserved for serious misconduct.
