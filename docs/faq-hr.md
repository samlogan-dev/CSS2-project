## Leave

### How do I apply for annual leave?

Log into the HR portal at `hr.internal`, navigate to **Leave > Apply**, and submit your request. Your line manager will receive an email to approve or decline. You will be notified of the outcome by email.

Leave must be requested with sufficient notice: 5 business days for 1–3 days, 10 business days for 4+ consecutive days. See `policy-leave.md` for full details.

### How much annual leave do I have?

Your current leave balance is visible in the HR portal under **Leave > My Balance**. Annual leave accrues from your start date. Full-time employees are entitled to 20 days per year; part-time employees accrue on a pro-rata basis.

### Can I take annual leave during my probation period?

Yes, leave accrues from Day 1. However, leave during the probation period is subject to manager approval and may be limited based on business needs.

### What happens to unused leave at the end of the year?

Up to 5 days of unused annual leave can be carried over to the next calendar year. Any balance above 5 days is forfeited on 31 December unless you have a written exemption approved by People & Culture. See `policy-leave.md` for details.

### I'm sick — what do I need to do?

Notify your line manager before 9:30 AM on the first day of absence. For absences of 3 or more consecutive days, you will need to provide a medical certificate. Submit it via the HR portal within 5 business days of returning. See `policy-leave.md` for the full sick leave policy.

### How do I apply for parental leave?

Contact People & Culture at people@company.com at least 8 weeks before your intended start date. We will send you the parental leave application form and walk you through the process. Primary carers receive 16 weeks of paid leave; secondary carers receive 4 weeks. See `policy-leave.md` for eligibility.

### Can I take leave without pay?

Yes, employees may apply for Leave Without Pay (LWOP) for periods up to 12 weeks. Your line manager and the Head of People & Culture must both approve. Note that leave does not accrue and some benefits may be affected during LWOP. See `policy-leave.md`.

## Pay and Benefits

### When is payday?

Salaries are paid on the 15th of each month. If the 15th falls on a weekend or public holiday, payment is made on the preceding business day.

### How do I update my bank account details?

Log into the HR portal and navigate to **My Details > Banking**. Changes must be submitted at least 5 business days before the next pay date to take effect that month.

### How do I access my payslips?

Payslips are available in the HR portal under **Pay > Payslips** after each pay run. You can download them as PDFs.

### What other benefits am I entitled to?

Refer to your employment contract for your specific benefit entitlements. Common benefits include:
- Mobile phone allowance (if applicable) — see `policy-expenses.md`.
- Remote work equipment allowance — see `policy-remote-work.md`.
- Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) — confidential counselling available to all employees. Contact people@company.com for access.
- Study leave — up to 5 days per year for approved professional development. See `policy-leave.md`.

## Conduct and Performance

### I want to raise a concern about a colleague's behaviour. What should I do?

You can speak to your line manager, contact People & Culture directly at people@company.com, or use the anonymous reporting tool in the HR portal. All reports are treated confidentially. See `policy-code-of-conduct.md` for more on the reporting process and how investigations are conducted.

### What is the performance review process?

Performance reviews are held twice a year — mid-year (June) and end-of-year (December). Your manager will initiate a self-assessment in the HR portal. Reviews include a structured conversation about performance against goals and career development.

## Administrative

### How do I update my personal details (address, emergency contact, etc.)?

Log into the HR portal and navigate to **My Details**. Updates take effect immediately.

### I'm leaving the company. What do I need to do?

Notify your manager and People & Culture as soon as your resignation is confirmed. People & Culture will send you an offboarding checklist. Key steps include: returning company equipment to IT, completing final expense claims, and arranging payout of outstanding leave.
